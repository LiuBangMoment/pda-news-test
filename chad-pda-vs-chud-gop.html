Committee Backs Ma for Chancellorship Despite GOP Disruptions

Despite a barrage of bigoted and unparliamentary attacks from the Group of the People, Usman Ma of the PDA outlined a unifying, forward-thinking economic agenda during his confirmation hearing.

By Jideofor Chidea · 3 Hours Ago
Usman Ma successfully advances his nomination, promising a balanced approach to Nigeria's economic future.

In a vital step forward for the Nigerian Republic, the Committee on Appropriations voted to recommend Usman Ma of the People's Democratic Alliance (PDA) for the pivotal role of Chancellor of Works. Ma, a Lagos resident with an extensive background consulting for high-tech industrial firms and a degree in economics from Tsinghua University, used the hearing to showcase exactly why he is fit for the Presidium. Through rigorous questioning from across the political spectrum, Ma articulated a comprehensive and unifying vision for the nation's economic future.

Before diving into policy, Ma thoroughly addressed concerns regarding potential conflicts of interest. Responding to W. Paul Olatunji of the Front for the Restoration of Integrity and Liberty in Nigeria, Ma assured the committee that his previous corporate work involved no ownership stakes, and his subsequent government service subjected him to strict anti-corruption scrutiny. He maintained that he holds no personal bias toward his former employers.

A Comprehensive Economic Vision

When questioned on his immediate priorities for his first 100 days by Salam! representative Musamil Haruna and MOTTAINAI's Ibitoye Atere, Ma outlined actionable, multi-lateral plans. He emphasized the necessity of building a proper economic apparatus, starting with passing a comprehensive budget and codifying the Central Bank. Immediate physical initiatives would include launching a housing program to address nationwide shortages and an infrastructure plan designed to link the North and South economically.

Addressing the future of Nigeria's industries, prompted by PDA colleague Kakakioluwa Liu, Ma championed a diversified approach. He highlighted biotechnology and high-tech manufacturing—pointing to native Nigerian electric cars—as sectors where the nation holds a special edge. Furthermore, he correctly identified that older industries like oil can be modernized for synthetics, while simultaneously championing the service industry, healthcare, and education.

Fiscal Responsibility and Socialist Market Economy

Ma expertly navigated ideological concerns regarding his economic models, particularly from the National Democratic Front's (NDF) Isaia Panduri, who questioned how socialism squares with a free market. Ma emphasized that his progressive vision does not seek to stifle enterprise, noting that market excesses can be remedied without hurting the market itself. "We are not pro-poverty, to get rich is glorious," Ma noted, firmly rejecting any notion that his policies are anti-growth.

"Socialism is simply an economy that works for the majority of the people. The free market is a powerful engine for growth."
— Usman Ma, Nominee for Chancellor of Works

Panduri also asked if Ma possessed the fortitude to make unpopular decisions. Ma affirmed his willingness to take tough actions, acknowledging that maintaining fiscal health might occasionally require reining in spending or implementing moderately high taxes. Expanding on macroeconomic strategy in response to Deputy Speaker Napoleon, Ma indicated that while a stable Central Bank structure is necessary, Nigeria's 15% unemployment rate requires maintaining some flexibility on inflation.

Sustainable Growth and Regional Integration

MOTTAINAI’s Ibitoye Atere pressed Ma on the vital intersections of development, environmental degradation, and regional African cooperation. Ma argued that environmental protection and economic growth are not a zero-sum game. He proposed utilizing technological advancements like biotech-enhanced crops and more efficient farming techniques to slow degradation, noting that building greener is entirely possible. He even envisioned a booming eco-tourism sector, suggesting tourists could travel to the Chad Zone to see Nigeria's growing rhino population in pristine grasslands.

Regarding foreign policy and African unity, Ma stated that regional development relies heavily on peace and mutually beneficial trade agreements. While acknowledging this falls heavily under the purview of the Chancellor of State, he suggested investigating the establishment of a regional development bank to foster broader African economic cooperation. When asked by Panduri if he would manage the country through ethnic quotas, Ma firmly stated he views Nigeria as a single economy and promised to work to make everyone prosperous.

Decorum Over Demagoguery

Unfortunately, the hearing's constructive dialogue was frequently sabotaged by Group of the People (GOP) representative Alhaji Jisa Gnodu. Acting completely outside the bounds of decorum, Gnodu hijacked the committee's time with a series of absurd, bigoted accusations. Gnodu repeatedly demanded to know if Ma was a member of the non-existent "Communist religion" due to his Chinese education.

The GOP representative then launched unprovoked homophobic slurs, explicitly questioning if Ma's "homosexuality" and "inclination to perverse personal behavior" would negatively impact his policy decisions. When Ma rightfully refused to entertain the unparliamentary accusations, Gnodu descended into screaming insults across the assembly floor, calling the nominee an "INFIDEL," a "DOG," and a "FIEND". The disruptive behavior reached such an unacceptable fever pitch that Deputy Speaker Napoleon was forced to temporarily remove Gnodu and another disruptive representative from the chamber to restore order.

Through it all, Usman Ma remained unflappable. Ultimately, the committee voted to pass the motion, choosing competence and progress over prejudice and disruption. However, Usman Ma's final appointment as Chancellor of Works is still pending approval by the full National Assembly.
