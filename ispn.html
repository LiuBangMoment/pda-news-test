The Strange Disappearance of ISPN Politicians and Their Equally Strange Return

Four representatives of the Islamic Socialist Party of Nigeria (ISPN) elected to the National Assembly have, for unknown reasons, not appeared at recent plenary sessions of parliament, and the Federal Capital Zone police departament have even declared them officially "missing."

By Hadiza Usaini · 1 Hour Ago
Local members of the Islamic Socialist Party of Nigeria.

The Islamic Socialist Party of Nigeria is a remarkably small faction, currently holding only four seats in the newly convened 2058 parliament. As a member of the newly formed Nigerian Progressive Coalition for Nature, People and Faith, the party has quickly developed a reputation for unreliability, with its MPs demonstrating a persistent tendency to skip vital parliamentary sessions.

This erratic attendance record was starkly highlighted during recent judicial and legislative proceedings. The four MPs entirely failed to vote on the crucial motion "On the Confirmation of Mr. Supreme Court as Chief Grand Justiciar of the High Court of the Republic". They did manage a brief parliamentary appearance to register a "Nay" vote on the Confirmation of Mr John Wakefield as Associate Justiciar of the High Court of the Republic. However, they subsequently vanished once more, failing to cast ballots on the Confirmation of Mr Benevolence Afolayan and the Confirmation of Mr Enock Rockwell — the latter of which ultimately failed — as well as completely missing the vote on the Advancement of Nigeria Upwards Bill.

The party’s dereliction of duty has extended beyond the voting lobby and into the administrative functioning of the National Assembly. The ISPN had to be repeatedly urged by the Speaker to appoint their allotted representatives to various parliamentary Committees. The mounting frustration ultimately culminated in a public reprimand, addressed as an open letter to the National Assembly.

"Islamic Socialist Party of Nigeria, just a warning, you haven't voted in the previous confirmation and you also still haven't appointed your members for the Rules and Ethics Committees. we won't keep waiting for you, so be aware we will force things through if you're the only vote left".

— Alexander Okafor, Speaker of the National Assembly

As the days passed with no communication, alarm bells began to ring across the political spectrum. Even their progressive coalition partners were completely unable to get in touch with the party's leadership, and their complete absence caused genuine concern amongst the authorities regarding their safety in the tense political climate of 2058. Then, just as abruptly as they had vanished, the politicians resurfaced. In the end, at a recent coalition meeting, Chairman Ali Balarabe of the ISPN casually appeared with a bewildering explanation: "Sorry if we have to be eaten... There's a LOT going on, man".

The bizarre reappearance has provided little clarity, and the true reasons for this prolonged absence remain the subject of intense, widespread speculation. Observers in the capital are left to wonder whether the disappearance was a deliberate, maximalist anti-parliamentary tactic designed to protest the new constitutional order, or simply the result of severe personal issues amongst the party's leadership. Until a formal explanation is offered, the broader public is left guessing whether there are other, unknown reasons behind the chaotic behaviour of the ISPN’s parliamentarians.
